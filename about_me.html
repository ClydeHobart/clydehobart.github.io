<About Me>

These images were taken from Google Maps

I was born in Bandera, TX, deep in the hill country, where I lived until I was 6, at which point I moved to Muskego, WI, where I lived and attended school from elementary through high school. During high school, I probably spent 95% of my life within the middle square, with my house (the little gray dot) to the east, my school to the west, my best friend's house to the south, and the local movie theater to the north--a visit to which was usually accompanied by a stop by the best fast food restaurant known to humankind, Taco Bell! Of course, in the fall of 2016 I moved to Hanover, NH, where I currently live now, attending Dartmouth College.

<My Interests>

I have many other interests in addition to software development, which include (but are not limited to):

  • Vocal Performance:

    When I first joined choir in 6th grade, I thought my voice was the bee's knees (but it definitely wasn't). Now, after 8 years, 3 choir classes, 2 co-curricular choirs (one being the Dartmouth Glee Club!), 2 all-male a cappela groups (one being the Dartmouth Brovertones!), and 1 term of private voice lessons, while I wouldn't say my voice is the bee's knees, I think I have a fairly firm grasp on vocal performance.

  • Skiing:

    I first learned how to ski in fifth grade, and since then it's been undeniably my favorite sport. I even was on the varsity ski team in high school for three years, but I have a lot more fun free skiing and going off jumps than I do on a race course. As you might expect, I was ecstatic to find out that Dartmouth has its own ski hill!

  • Twisty Puzzles:

    It might sound like a dumb name (honestly it somewhat is), but "twisty puzzles" is the category of puzzles like the Rubik's Cube that scramble by rotating pieces around a set of axes. I get a great deal of enjoyment from solving them, and so I have a collection of about a dozen or so that I solve when I'm bored. The one I've solved the most (aside from a normal 3x3 Rubik's Cube) is my Sheng Shou 10x10x10, and my favorite one is probably my Pentultimate, mostly because I find its geometry beautiful (its pieces slide along the great circles of an icosidodecahedron).

  • Video Games:

    I've been playing video games for years, and they've helped me get through some rough times. Video games have also been one of the main contributing factors to me studying Computer Science: I want to be a video game programmer after Dartmouth. When I first discovered this occupation after talking to a friend during winter break during eighth grade, I realized that being a video game programmer was a very suitable position for me, someone who enjoys working with technology, problem solving, and video games. And so, this dream has been a driving force for me for years.
